Christmas

The season of joy, love, and giving.

Traditions of Christmas

Christmas traditions blend Christian observances of Jesus' birth with secular customs like decorating trees, exchanging gifts, baking cookies, singing carols, and festive meals, often involving Santa Claus, stockings, and lights, with variations globally like KFC dinners in Japan or picnics in warm climates, celebrating themes of family, generosity, and hope.

Common Secular Traditions

  • Decorating: Setting up and decorating Christmas trees with lights and ornaments, hanging wreaths, and displaying festive lights and figurines
  • Gifts & Santa: Exchanging presents, leaving cookies and milk for Santa, and writing letters to him.
  • Food & Drink: Baking and decorating cookies (like gingerbread), making festive drinks (like eggnog), and having special holiday meals.
  • Entertainment: Watching Christmas movies, listening to carols, ice skating, and wearing "ugly" sweaters.
  • Decorations: Hanging stockings, mistletoe, and using candles in windows.
