Hanukkah

The season of miracles.

Traditions of Hanukkah

Hanukkah traditions center on the Festival of Lights, featuring nightly menorah lighting (adding one candle each night), playing dreidel, singing songs, giving gelt (chocolate coins), and eating foods fried in oil like latkes (potato pancakes) and sufganiyot (donuts), all commemorating the miracle of the Temple oil lasting eight days.

Common Secular Traditions

  • Menorah Lighting (Hanukkiah): A nine-branched candelabra used to light one additional candle each night, using the helper candle (shamash) to light the others, symbolizing the miracle.
  • Foods Fried in Oil: Eating latkes and sufganiyot (jelly donuts) to remember the oil.
  • Dreidel Game: Playing with a four-sided spinning top marked with Hebrew letters (Nun, Gimel, Hey, Shin).
  • Gelt: Giving children chocolate coins, often used in the dreidel game
  • Songs & Prayers: Reciting special blessings and singing songs like Ma'oz Tzur.
