Winter Holidays

Winter holidays include religious and cultural celebrations such as Christmas, Hanukkah, Kwanzaa, and Diwali, as well as New Year's Eve and Day. Other traditions observed include the Winter Solstice, Lunar New Year, and Boxing Day. These holidays are celebrated globally and include a variety of customs like lighting candles, exchanging gifts, and feasting.

Common Secular Traditions

Winter holiday traditions globally involve themes of light, renewal, and community, featuring celebrations like Christmas (decorating trees, gift-giving), Hanukkah (menorah lighting, latkes), Diwali (festival of lights, rangoli), Kwanzaa (seven principles), and Solstice/Yule (Yule log, evergreen decor), alongside secular customs like ice skating, baking cookies, festive lights, and charitable giving, all fostering joy and connection.
